How New Yorkers spend more on eating out than on shopping

Scrolling story ¬∑ data + everyday habits

What I wanted to understand

I live in New York City, where it sometimes feels like people spend more on eating out than on actual products. I wanted to see if that feeling connects to how the city is built and how people live in it.

Instead of just guessing, I looked at three simple pieces of city data:

  • Income level: how higher income changes spending.
  • Restaurant density: how many places to eat are around.
  • Apartment size: how much space people have for stuff.

Hypothesis

People in New York City spend more money on eating out than on shopping because they earn more, have restaurants everywhere, and live in small apartments with limited storage.
